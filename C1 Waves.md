Simple Harmonic Motion occurs when acceleration is oppositely proportional to displacement from equilibrium point:
- Equation form: $a = -\omega^2 x$
- Word form: acceleration is proportional in magnitude to displacement from equilibrium point and opposite in direction

SHM can be described using time period $T$, frequency $f$, angular frequency $\omega$, amplitude, equilibrium position, and displacement

Time period $T = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{2\pi}{\omega}$:
- Mass-spring system given by $T = 2\pi \sqrt{\cfrac{m}{k}}$
- Simple pendulum given by $T = 2\pi \sqrt{\cfrac{l}{g}}$

$E_T = EPE + KE$
- $EPE = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$
- $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$

- Three initial points, then cross at half energy

A particle undergoing SHM can be described using:
- $x = x_0 \sin{(\omega t + \phi)}$
- $v = \omega x_0 \cos{(\omega t + \phi)}$
- $v = \pm \omega \sqrt{x_0^2-x^2}$
- $E_T = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2x_0^2$
- $E_P = \frac{1}{2} m\omega^2 x^2$
