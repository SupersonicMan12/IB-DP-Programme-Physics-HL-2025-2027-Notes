
Prerequisites from primary school:
- Solids, liquids and gases
- Density $\rho = \frac{m}{V}$
- Kelvin and Celsius degree scales

Prerequisites from middle school:
- $\overline{E_k} = \frac{3}{2}k_BT$
- $E_k = \frac{3}{2}k_B NT = \frac{3}{2}nRT$
- Total internal energy $U = E_k + E_p$, where $E_p$ is the energy from the forces between the molecules

Temperature difference determines direction of resultant thermal energy transfer

Phase change is a change in $E_p$, while temperature change is a change in $E_k$:
$Q = mc\Delta T$ while $Q = mL$

Conduction, Convection and Thermal Radiation are the three ways for TE transfer:
- Conduction = difference in KE of particles: $$\frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t} = kA\frac{\Delta T}{\Delta x}$$
- Convection = fluid density differences causing fluid bulk motion
- Radiation is the result of the emission of EM waves from surface of body, with  $$L = \sigma A T^4$$
	- Extending, brightness is defined by $$b = \frac{L}{4\pi d^2}$$
	- Emission spectrum of a black body has peak of $\lambda_{max}$ satisfying $$\lambda_{max}T = 2.9\times 10^{-3} \, \text{mK}$$
Notice how luminosity $L$ is in Jules of energy while $b$ is Jules per meter squared of intensity.
