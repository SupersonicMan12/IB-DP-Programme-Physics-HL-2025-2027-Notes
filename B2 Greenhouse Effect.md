Emissivity is the ratio of per unit area power radiation between a body and an ideal black body:
$$e = \text{emissivity} = \frac{\text{power radiated per area}}{\sigma T^4}$$
Albedo is the fraction of energy reflected off a macroscopic system $$\alpha = \text{albedo} = \frac{\text{total scattered power}}{\text{total incident power}}$$
Solar constant intensity received on earth $$S = b = \frac{\sigma A T^4}{4\pi d^2} = \sigma T^4 \left(\frac{R_S}{d}\right)^2 = 1370 \; \text W/m^2$$
To get energy, multiply by area. The mean incoming intensity is $S/4$ because nights exist.

Methane CH4, Water H2O, Carbon Dioxide CO2 and Nitrous Oxide N2O are the four main greenhouse gases generated by natural and human activity. They **absorb** IR radiation that the earth reflects and **emit** that energy in all directions, returning around half of the energy back into the atmosphere.

Why do they absorb IR? 
1. **Natural frequency** of the greenhouse gases include the IR range, causing **resonance**
2. Lights have **photons**; if photon matches the gap, the photon is absorbed to increase the molecule's energy; emission also by photon, decrease energy
