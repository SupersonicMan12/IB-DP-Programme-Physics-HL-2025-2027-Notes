## From classical model → nuclear model

> [!NOTE]
>  **Rutherford (Geiger–Marsden) scattering**: $\alpha$ particles fired at thin gold foil  
>  
>Observations:
>   - Most pass straight through → atom mostly empty  
>   - Some deflect slightly → positive charge present  
>   - Very few deflect strongly/backscatter → **tiny dense nucleus**
>  
> Conclusions:
>   - Mass concentrated in small nucleus  
>   - Positive charge concentrated in nucleus  
>   - Electrons occupy surrounding space  
## Nuclear structure & notation

- Atom = nucleus (protons + neutrons) + electrons  
- Nuclear notation:
$${}^A_ZX$$

- $Z$ = proton number (defines element)  
- $A$ = nucleon number  
- Neutrons = $A - Z$

| Particle | Mass (kg)              | Charge (C) |
| -------- | ---------------------- | ---------- |
| Proton   | $1.673\times 10^{-27}$ | e          |
| Neutron  | $1.675\times 10^{-27}$ | 0          |
| Electron | $9.110\times10^{-31}$  | -e         |
## Discrete energy levels

> [!CORE IDEA]
> Energy in atoms is **quantized**

- Electrons occupy fixed energy levels  
- No values in between  
- Going up is absorb, going down is emit

## Photon emission & absorption

> [!About Photons]
$$E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$$

- Energy is released and absorbed by the atom through the mission and absorption of photons
- Electron transitions:
$$\Delta E = hf$$

- If electron drops → photon emitted  
- If electron rises → photon absorbed  
## Spectra → direct evidence of quantization

- Emission spectrum:
  - Bright lines  
- Absorption spectrum:
  - Dark lines  

These **provide evidence for discrete atomic energy level**.
- Each element has **unique spectral fingerprint** 
- Therefore:
  - Spectra prove **discrete energy levels**
## Deviations from Rutherford Scattering 
![[Screenshot 2026-04-17 at 08.25.36.png]]
Rutherford expected the number of scattered particles to decrease as energy increases. However, it bends:
- The alpha particles got sufficiently close:
> [!NOTE] Closest Approach
> $$E = k\frac{qQ}{d}$$
> $$d_{min} = k\frac{qQ}{E}$$
- Another force must have affected the particles:
- The strong nuclear force acts within the nucleus only: 100 times stronger but only within the range of $10^{-15}$ m.


## Nuclear radius

$$R = R_0 A^{1/3}$$

- Larger nuclei scale with $A^{1/3}$  , $A$ is nucleon number; $R_0$ is Fermi Radius = $1.20 \times 10^{-15}$
- Implies: nuclear density is roughly constant 
## Angular momentum quantization

Niels Bohr:
$$L = mvr$$

- standing waves exist around the nucleus: wave-particle duality
- Taking $\lambda  = \frac{h}{mv}$, $\frac{2\pi r}{\lambda} = n$, then $mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}$.
