Moving source:
$$f' = f\left(\cfrac{v}{v\pm u_s}\right)$$
Moving observer:
$$f' = f\left(\cfrac{v\pm u_o}{v}\right)$$
For light, just memorize
$$\cfrac{\Delta f}{f} = \cfrac{\Delta \lambda}{\lambda} = \cfrac{v}{c}$$
For distant galaxies, shifts in spectral lines provide information about moving bodies: redshift means it is moving away, blueshift means it is moving closer.
