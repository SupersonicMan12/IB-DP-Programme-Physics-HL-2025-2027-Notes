Torque $\tau$ of a force is $\tau = Fr\sin{\theta}$

Now everything else is just the same as linear, analogous to it:

Rotational equilibrium: resultant $\tau$ = 0
If unbalanced net torque, there's angular acceleration
Describing rotational motion:
- $\Delta \theta = \cfrac{\omega_f+\omega_i}{2}t$
- $\omega_f = \omega_i + \alpha t$
- $\Delta \theta = \omega_it + \frac{1}{2}\alpha t^2$
- $\omega_f^2 - \omega_i^2 = 2\alpha \Delta \theta$
Moment of inertia is the equivalent to mass in linear motion, given by $I = \Sigma mr^2$
Newton's second law of rotation: $\tau = I \alpha$
Angular momentum $L = I\omega$
Angular impulse $\Delta L = \Delta (I\omega) = \tau \Delta t$, where $\tau$ is average torque
Kinetic energy of rotational motion $E_k = \frac{1}{2} I\omega^2 = \frac{L^2}{2I}$
