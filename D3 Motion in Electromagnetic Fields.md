## Charged Particle in Electric Field
Motion of a charged particle in a uniform electric field:
- Force on charge $q$: $F = qE$, giving acceleration $a = \frac{qE}{m}$
- Positive charges accelerate in the direction of $E$; negative charges accelerate opposite to $E$
- Parallel entry to field lines → uniformly accelerated straight-line motion
- Perpendicular entry to field lines → **parabolic path** (horizontal velocity unchanged, accelerates along field direction), analogous to projectile motion

Work done by electric field on charge $q$ through potential difference $\Delta V_e$: $$W = q\Delta V_e$$
- Relates to kinetic energy: $q\Delta V_e = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 - \frac{1}{2}mu^2$
- A charge accelerated from rest through $\Delta V$: $v = \sqrt{\frac{2q\Delta V}{m}}$

## Charged Particle in a Magnetic Field: 

Force on a charge moving in a magnetic field (**Lorentz force**): $$F = qvB\sin\theta$$
- Above for magnitude; $\theta$ is the angle between $\vec{v}$ and $\vec{B}$
- Direction given by right-hand rule: fingers point along $\vec{v}$, curl toward $\vec{B}$, thumb gives $\vec{F}$ for positive charges; reverse for negative charges
- Force is always perpendicular to velocity → does no work → kinetic energy is constant → speed is constant!!!
#### Circular Motion

Circular motion in uniform $B$ (when $v \perp B$):
- Magnetic force provides centripetal force: $qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$
- Rearranging, radius of circular path: $$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$
- Period: $T = \frac{2\pi r}{v} = \frac{2\pi m}{qB}$ (independent of speed)
- Increasing mass or speed → larger radius; increasing charge or $B$ → smaller radius
- Positive and negative charges orbit in opposite directions
## Velocity Selectors

Velocity selector (crossed $E$ and $B$ fields):
- Electric and magnetic forces oppose each other: $qE = qvB$
- Undeflected when: $v = \frac{E}{B}$
- Particles with $v > \frac{E}{B}$ deflect toward $B$ force; $v < \frac{E}{B}$ deflect toward $E$ force
- Combined with a second $B$ field region → mass spectrometer: separates particles by $\frac{q}{m}$

Charge-to-mass ratio: $$\frac{q}{m} = \frac{v}{rB}$$
- Measured by observing the radius of a particle's circular path in known $B$ and $v$
- Used in mass spectrometers and bubble chamber analysis
## Current in Magnetic Field

Force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field (**Ampère's force**): $$F = BIL\sin\theta$$
- $\theta$ is angle between current direction and $\vec{B}$
- Direction: right-hand rule with fingers along $I$, curl toward $\vec{B}$, thumb gives $\vec{F}$
- Principle behind **DC motors**: current-carrying coil in $B$ experiences a torque; commutators reverse current each half-turn for continuous rotation

Magnetic field due to a long straight wire: $$B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r}$$
- $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}$ T m A$^{-1}$ is permeability of free space

Force per unit length between two parallel current-carrying wires: $$\frac{F}{L} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi r}$$
- Currents in same direction → attractive force
- Currents in opposite directions → repulsive force
- Each wire sits in the magnetic field created by the other
