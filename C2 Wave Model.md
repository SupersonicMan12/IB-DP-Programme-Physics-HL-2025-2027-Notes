Wave = disturbance that transfers energy without net transfer of matter

Transverse and Longitudinal Traveling Waves:
- Transverse = direction of motion of particles and direction of energy transfer are perpendicular
- Longitudinal = direction of motion of particles and direction of energy transfer are parallel
- $v = f\lambda = \frac{\lambda}{T}$
- Sound waves = longitudinal, needs an air medium to travel as a mechanical wave
- EM waves = transverse 

Path difference = $\Delta x$
Phase difference = $\Delta \phi = 2\pi \frac{\Delta t}{T} = 2\pi \frac{\Delta x}{\lambda}$

Long: rarefaction and compression
Transverse: crest and compression

Wavefront = same phase
