From the Conservation of Energy, we have the **First Law of Thermodynamics** $$\Delta U = Q - W,$$ where $W$ is work done by the system and $Q$ is the heat input to the system:
- $W = \int P dV = P \Delta V$
- $\Delta U = \Delta (\frac{3}{2} Nk_B \Delta T) = \Delta (\frac{3}{2} Nk_B \Delta T)$

Entropy $S$ is the degree of disorder of the particles of a system:
- Macro: $\Delta S = \frac{\Delta Q}{T}$
- Micro: $\Delta S = k_B \ln{\Omega}$ where $\Omega$ is the number of possible microstates of the system

**Second Law of Thermodynamics**: any process in the universe has $\Delta S \ge 0$

In the real world, $\Delta S > 0$, or irreversible

Four processes:
- Isovolumetric
- Isobaric
- Isothermal
- Adiabatic: $PV^{5/3} = c$

Cyclic gas processes are used to run heat engines, whose efficiency is $$\eta = \frac{\text{useful}}{\text{input}}$$
Carnot cycle gives the theoretical limit of the above process, with $$\eta_{Carnot} = 1-\frac{T_c}{T_h}$$
